# BD Pipeline Outreach Email Templates

Five templates for different trigger types. All under 150 words. Each ends with one sharp question.

---

## Template 1: Safety Hiring Surge

**Trigger**: Target company posted 3+ AUTOSAR / ISO 26262 / functional safety roles in the last 30 days.

**Subject**: Safety scaling at [Company] — ASPICE evidence question

**Body**:

Hi [First Name],

Noticed [Company] is actively hiring for functional safety and AUTOSAR roles — looks like a significant ramp.

One thing that tends to catch teams off guard at that scale: the ASPICE evidence infrastructure. Generating compliant work products for an ASIL-D gate requires more than headcount — it needs process baselines, traceability chains, and tool qualification in place before the program review.

At Thinking in Code, we take workstream ownership on safety-critical deliverables — not just consulting support. We've done this at Nordic Auto and Garrett with direct ASIL-D responsibility.

What specific safety deliverables are you most concerned about hitting before your next program gate?

Best,
Daniel Turcu
Thinking in Code

Is there a safety-critical workstream on this program that's currently unowned or at risk of slipping a gate?

## Template 4: ASPICE or ISO 26262 Mention in Press / Job Posts

**Trigger**: Company mentioned ASPICE, ISO 26262, or cybersecurity compliance in job postings or press releases.

**Subject**: ASPICE at [Company] — process baseline question

**Body**:

Hi [First Name],

Noticed [Company] is putting emphasis on ASPICE and ISO 26262 compliance — both in job posts and public-facing content.

Process compliance at ASPICE L2 and above is usually straightforward to declare but harder to sustain across an active delivery program. The gap tends to show up during audits when evidence doesn't trace cleanly to actual work products.

We did a full ASPICE L2 process audit for Hella — identified gaps, built the evidence infrastructure, and supported the re-audit. That's a concrete workstream, not consulting advice.

Where is your current ASPICE compliance relative to your next audit checkpoint?
